## Re-Audit Notes

The signed assurance packet remains a pure no-authority artifact:
`canAdmit = false`, `activatesEnforcement = false`, and `autoEnforce = false`.
The new expected fingerprint field prevents a caller from using a fresh
self-generated public key as its own trust anchor for local `ed25519` packet
signatures.

The trusted fingerprint may come from an operator-pinned key, PKI leaf
certificate, JWKS, or another trusted key-distribution channel. The packet
builder does not fetch that trust material and does not claim complete PKI
validation.
